📱 Opening the app + signing in

1

Open the Free Roof Inspections app on your phone

Tap the link the office texted you, or open free-roof-inspections.netlify.app in your phone's browser. Add it to your home screen so it opens like a real app.

2

Pick your name

First time only — you'll see a list of all active inspectors. Tap your name. The app remembers you on this phone, so you won't have to do this again unless you "Switch user."

Don't see your name? Your manager needs to mark you "active" and you need to finish your setup link (confirm your home address). Text your manager.

🎯 Claiming an inspection

3

Look at "Available near me"

Every signed inspection that isn't claimed yet shows up here, sorted by distance from your home base. Each card shows the homeowner, address, and how far away it is.

4

Tap "Claim" on the one you want

The job is now assigned to you. It moves to your "My active claims" list and disappears from everyone else's "Available."

⏰ Claim, then go. If you claim a job and don't finish it by end-of-day, the system auto-releases it back to the pool and your manager gets pinged. Don't claim jobs you can't get to today.

📷 The 6-step photo wizard

When you arrive at the property, open the inspection from "My active claims" and tap Start inspection. The app walks you through each photo, one screen at a time.

1

📷 House number

Stand on the sidewalk or driveway. Take one clear, in-focus photo of the house number on the front of the house (or on the mailbox). Confirms we're at the right address.

2

📷 Front of the house

Back up far enough to see the whole front of the house in the frame — roof + walls + driveway. One photo, straight-on if possible.

3

🏠 How many stories?

Tap 1 story or 2 stories. Count the floors of the house, not the roof slopes. Ranch = 1. Bedrooms upstairs = 2.

If 2 stories: You'll do the 1st-floor roof first (all 4 sides), then climb up and do the 2nd-floor roof the same way. The app will tell you when to climb.
4

📷 Roof overview — from the FAR LEFT

Climb up. Walk all the way to the far left of the roof. From there, take one wide photo capturing as much of the roof as you can see. This is the "before we zoom in" shot.

5

📷 Each slope — overview, then close-ups

You'll work clockwise from the LEFT side: left → front → right → rear. For each side:

  • Tell the app how many slopes are on that side
  • For each slope:
    • One overview photo — stand back, capture the whole slope in one shot
    • Detail photos — move in close, take as many as needed of anything you see (hail strikes, lifted shingles, granule loss, exposed nails, soft spots, wear marks)

What "close-up detail" should capture

  • Hail strikes / round dents in shingles
  • Lifted, torn, or missing shingles
  • Bare spots where granules are gone
  • Exposed nail heads
  • Mossy or soft spots underfoot
  • Flashing pulled away from valleys, chimneys, vents
6

If 2-story: do it all again on the 2nd-floor roof

When the 1st floor is finished, the app shows a green ✅ "1st floor finished" screen. Climb up to the 2nd-floor roof and tap continue. Same sequence: overview from far left, then slopes clockwise.

🧾 Picking the result

After the last slope, the app asks: what did you find? Pick one of three.

🚨 Damage — file PA claim

Clear storm damage — hail strikes you can see, wind-lifted or torn shingles, missing pieces. This triggers the PA (Public Adjuster) claim path — homeowner's insurance gets a packet with all your photos.

💰 Retail — homeowner pays

Significant wear & tear but no storm damage. The homeowner pays for replacement out of pocket.

You'll be asked for 10 more close-up photos of the worst spots before submitting — show the worn-out areas in detail so the sales rep can have a real conversation about it.

✓ No damage

Roof is sound, nothing to file. Homeowner gets a free no-damage certificate showing the roof has 5+ years of life — protects them from "your roof is too old" letters from their insurance.

Honest is always right. If you're not sure between Retail and Damage, look back at your detail photos. Hail strikes = Damage. Bare granule patches with no impact marks = Retail. Don't force a category — your photos tell the truth.

💡 Photo & safety tips

Good photos = fewer callbacks

  • Tap the part of the screen you want in focus before you take the shot. Phones default to focusing on the center, which is usually sky.
  • Shoot with the sun behind you when you can — backlit photos hide hail strikes.
  • For close-ups, get within arm's reach. Phones can't zoom and stay sharp — physically move closer.
  • Steady your phone against your forearm. A blurry detail photo is a useless detail photo.

Stay safe up there

  • If the roof feels soft or wet, get off. Mark as Retail or No Damage from the ground if you have to — your safety is the only thing.
  • Never inspect in active rain or lightning.
  • Watch for power lines, skylights, and rotted edges.
  • If you can't safely access part of the roof, photograph it from the ground or the ladder — the app accepts any photo. Note in your detail photos that you couldn't access X.

🛟 When something goes wrong

I claimed the wrong inspection

From your "My active claims" list, tap the inspection and look for "Release back to pool." That puts it back in everyone's "Available."

The app froze / I lost reception mid-inspection

Photos you've already taken in this session are stored on your phone, not lost. Close the app, reopen it, and the wizard picks back up where you left off. Submit when you have signal again.

I took a bad photo by accident

Tap the ✕ on the photo thumbnail to remove it before tapping the green "Got it — next" button. Once you move to the next step, that photo is locked in.

I'm there but the homeowner isn't home

You can still do the full external inspection — you don't need anyone to let you in. Take the photos, submit the result, and the office handles the follow-up call.
