# DoorDispatcher — Rep Training Video

**Format:** Screen recording of the real tool + AI avatar in the corner (HeyGen)
**Length target:** 5–8 minutes
**Audience:** New reps, day one
**Voice:** Friendly, confident trainer. Short sentences. Talk *to* the rep ("you"), not about them.

**How to use this script:**
- **[SCREEN]** = what the viewer sees / what to record.
- **[SAY]** = the exact narration for the AI avatar/voice. Paste these lines straight into HeyGen.
- Keep the avatar small in a corner; the screen is the star. Reps learn by watching the taps.

---

## SCENE 1 — Welcome (0:00–0:25)

**[SCREEN]** Open on the DoorDispatcher, zoomed to a neighborhood full of pins. Rep's name and level badge visible top-right.

**[SAY]**
"Welcome to the DoorDispatcher — this is the tool you'll use every day to knock smart, not hard. It shows you exactly which doors to hit, keeps you off doors your teammates already worked, and books your appointments straight into JobNimbus. Let me walk you through a normal day, start to finish. It's easier than it looks."

---

## SCENE 2 — What the pins mean (0:25–1:05)

**[SCREEN]** Slowly pan the map. As each color is named, briefly tap that chip in the "Pins to show" panel so only that color highlights.

**[SAY]**
"First, the colors. Every pin is a house, and the color tells you its story.
- **Blue** dots are your fresh leads — Instant Quotes and marketing leads that nobody's touched.
- **Pink** are no-sits that need to be rescheduled — someone booked, it fell through, go get it back.
- **Green** is a booked appointment.
- **Black** means not interested — skip it.
- And **baby blue** means a door already worked today, so you don't re-knock your buddy's house.

On the right, the 'Pins to show' list lets you turn any color on or off. Tap 'All' to see everything, or focus on just the work you want."

---

## SCENE 3 — Starting your day WITH an appointment (1:05–2:20)

**[SCREEN]** Open the map fresh. The purple banner appears at top: *"You have 1 appointment today — first at 11:00 AM. Plan your doors around it?"* Tap **Plan my day →**. The time sheet opens; tap **Build my plan**. The route draws — green numbered stops around the appointment.

**[SAY]**
"Here's the best part. If you've got an appointment today, the map knows. The second you open it, you'll see this banner up top telling you what time your appointment is.
Tap 'Plan my day,' pick your start time, and hit 'Build my plan.'
Watch what happens — the map builds your whole day *around* that appointment. It fills in doors to knock before you're due, keeps you close so you're never far when it's time, and then keeps you knocking after until eight PM. You just follow the stops."

**[SCREEN]** Tap the first stop, show the arrival, then tap **Appt done** at the appointment.

**[SAY]**
"When you finish your appointment, tap 'Appt done,' and the map re-plans the rest of your day from wherever you're standing, right then. Run long? It trims doors. Finish early? It adds them. It's always working off the real clock."

---

## SCENE 4 — Starting your day WITHOUT an appointment (2:20–3:00)

**[SCREEN]** Fresh map, no banner. Tap **Route an area**. Drag a box around a neighborhood. The route draws through the doors inside it.

**[SAY]**
"No appointment today? Even easier. Tap 'Route an area,' then just drag a box around the neighborhood you want to work. The map instantly routes every workable door inside that box in the most efficient driving order — no zig-zagging, no doubling back. Then you just start at stop one and go."

---

## SCENE 5 — Working a door (3:00–4:00)

**[SCREEN]** Tap a stop. The door card opens with the address and status buttons. Show tapping a status (e.g. "Not home," then a positive one).

**[SAY]**
"When you get to a door, tap it. You'll see the address and every way to status it. Knocked and nobody home? Tap 'Not home.' Not interested? Tap it, and that door goes dark so nobody knocks it again.
Everything you tap is saved instantly and shared with your team and your manager — live. No paperwork, no end-of-day data entry."

**[SCREEN]** Show the "came back / re-status" case: a door marked "Not home" that you tap again and change.

**[SAY]**
"And if you marked a house 'not home' but then they come to the door — no problem. As long as you're on your route, just tap that pin again and re-status it. It's never locked in."

---

## SCENE 6 — Booking an appointment / signing an inspection (4:00–4:50)

**[SCREEN]** On a door card, tap the schedule/appointment action. Show the appointment being set. Then show **Sign Inspection** opening the prefilled Claim Intake.

**[SAY]**
"When you book someone, tap 'Schedule appointment,' set the time, and it lands in JobNimbus automatically — assigned to you, on the contact and the job, so the phone number and email stay with you.
And when a homeowner's ready to sign right there on the door? Tap 'Sign Inspection.' The claim form opens already filled in with their address — you just confirm and sign. The pin turns to 'Sold' the moment you're done."

---

## SCENE 7 — Found a damaged roof that isn't on the map (Self-Gen) (4:50–5:40)

**[SCREEN]** Tap the drop-a-pin button on an empty spot. The "Owner occupied?" check runs. Show the result, then the Sign / Retail / Pending options.

**[SAY]**
"Out knocking and you spot a beat-up roof that isn't pinned? Drop your own pin right on it.
The map instantly checks the county records to tell you if the owner actually lives there — so you know if you're talking to the decision-maker before you knock.
From there you can sign them up, set it as a retail lead, or mark it pending to come back. It goes into JobNimbus as a self-generated lead, credited to you."

---

## SCENE 8 — Go-backs (5:40–6:20)

**[SCREEN]** Open the "Today's go-backs" card. Show the dated go-back pins and the Damage / No-Damage / Retail inline actions.

**[SAY]**
"After an inspection, some houses need a second trip — a damage go-back, a no-damage follow-up, or a retail come-back. The map keeps those in your 'Today's go-backs' list so nothing slips.
Tap one, and you can handle it right from the card. Everything stays organized so you always know your next move."

---

## SCENE 9 — Wrap up (6:20–7:00)

**[SCREEN]** Zoom out to show a full worked day — a mix of statused pins and a completed route.

**[SAY]**
"And that's your day. Open the map, follow your plan, tap each door as you go. The map handles the routing, the tracking, and the JobNimbus paperwork — so all you have to do is knock, talk, and close.
Get out there and go get 'em."

---

## Production notes for the video tool

1. **Record the screen clips first** (real tool, one flow per scene). I can capture these for you.
2. **Paste the [SAY] lines** into HeyGen as the avatar's script, scene by scene.
3. Put the **avatar small in a corner** (bottom-right), screen full behind it.
4. Add simple **on-screen text labels** at each step ("Tap 'Route an area'") — HeyGen has a text tool. Reps skim.
5. Keep transitions instant. No fancy wipes; this is a how-to, not a movie.
6. Optional: a 3-second title card at the start ("DoorDispatcher — Daily Walkthrough") and an end card with a support number.
